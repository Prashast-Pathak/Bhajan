Topic Intro: Patience in Hindu philosophy is not passive waiting — it is titiksha (forbearance), the active quality of continuing to act rightly in the face of obstacles. The Gita's teaching on the steady-minded sage (sthitaprajna) is essentially a portrait of supreme patience: someone who remains undisturbed in difficulty and unelated in success.
